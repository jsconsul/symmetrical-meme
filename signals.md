## Strong Buying / Need Signals

- They explicitly mention a problem they’re trying to solve.
- They post job openings for roles related to your service/product (e.g., “Hiring DevOps Engineer” → they have infrastructure issues).
- They publicly request recommendations or vendors.
- They announce new funding rounds.
- They mention upcoming system upgrades or migrations.
- They talk about scaling, expansion, or rapid user growth.
- They’re adopting relevant technologies that align with what you offer.
- They post about budget approval or capital allocation.
- They mention compliance concerns or regulatory changes.
- They talk about abandoning current tools or dissatisfaction with their vendors.
- They publicly request recommendations or vendors for a specific solution, or mention RFPs/tender processes.
- Hiring Specific Pain: They post job openings for roles directly related to solving the pain point your service/product addresses (e.g., “Hiring DevOps Engineer” -> infrastructure issues; “Hiring Head of Compliance” -> regulatory gaps).
- New Funds & Allocation: They announce new funding rounds (Series A, B, etc.) or post about budget approval or capital allocation for specific projects.
- System Overhaul Imminent: They mention upcoming system upgrades, migrations, or replacing "legacy infrastructure.
- "Clear Dissatisfaction: They talk about abandoning current tools or express clear dissatisfaction with their incumbent vendors or providers.
- Compliance/Regulatory Deadline: They mention specific compliance concerns or regulatory changes (e.g., GDPR, CCPA) that require a solution by a specific deadline.
- Scaling Crisis: They talk about rapid user growth, scaling challenges, or express clear operational stress due to their current limits.
- Targeted Tech Adoption: They're explicitly seeking integration partners for a major, new technology they are aggressively adopting (e.g., "seeking implementation partners for Snowflake/Azure/Generative AI").

## Moderate Signals

- They consistently publish posts on topics related to your offering (pain points in your domain).
- They engage with competitor content or thought leaders in your space.
- They ask questions publicly that relate to operational challenges.
- Their comments sections are filled with complaints from customers or users.
- They show interest in industry innovation or emerging tech.
- They hint at product roadmap shifts or strategic redirection.
- Their leadership team publishes posts about process improvements or efficiency initiatives.
- Their marketing pushes more aggressively than usual (indicates growth mode).
- They increase frequency of recruiting posts in a specific skill domain.
- They attend or speak at events related to your field.
- Consistent Content Focus: They consistently publish posts on a specific technical topic or pain point that aligns with your solution (shows rising internal focus).
- Competitor Engagement: They engage with content from your direct competitors or key thought leaders in your specific domain (shows active research).
- Strategic Direction Hints: They hint at product roadmap shifts or a strategic redirection that will require new tooling (e.g., "moving from B2C to B2B").
- Leadership Efficiency Drive: Their leadership team publishes posts about process improvements, efficiency initiatives, or cost management (indicates an internal mandate for change).
- Recruiting Volume Spike: They show a significant increase in the frequency of recruiting posts in a specific skill domain (e.g., hiring 10+ Backend Engineers) indicating a major project kickoff.
- Public Operational Questions: They ask questions publicly (on LinkedIn/Twitter) that relate to operational challenges or seek "best practices" for infrastructure or service delivery.
- Specific Event Attendance: They announce they are attending or speaking at events specifically related to your field (shows focused intent, not general marketing).

## Weak Signals

- They mention general company growth or “exciting times ahead”.
- Their CEO or leadership becomes more active on social media.
- Vague partnerships announcements with no details.
- They post feel-good corporate culture content (team outings, celebrations).
- They’re re-sharing broader industry news.
- They publish generic company milestones (“10 years anniversary”, “ISO certification”).
- They post motivational quotes or thought fluff.
- They congratulate other companies.
- They talk about market trends without relating them to internal changes.
- They are building brand awareness but not operational depth.
- General Growth Buzz: They mention general company growth or "exciting times ahead" without specifics on budget or projects.
- Vague Partnerships: Vague partnerships announcements with no details on the scope or technology involved.
- Leadership Activity Increase: Their CEO or leadership becomes more active on social media without sharing strategic details (often driven by PR).
- Feel-Good Content: They post primarily corporate culture content (team outings, celebrations, holidays).
- Industry News Re-Share: They are re-sharing broader industry news or market trends without relating them to internal changes or projects.
- Generic Milestones: They publish generic company milestones ("10 years anniversary," "opening new regional satellite office").
- Market Trend Talk: They talk about broad market trends (e.g., "The future of AI") but without relating them to internal changes or solutions.
- Brand Awareness Posts: Their posts are solely focused on building brand awareness but lack operational depth or technical details.

## Hidden / Pattern-Based Signals

- They shift posting tone from brand voice to problem-focused messaging.
- Mentions of internal overwork or understaffing → operational stress.
- Sudden silence after active posting → internal chaos or restructuring.
- Increase in comments from employees (“We’re hiring fast!”, “Crazy growth right now!”).
- Leadership reshuffles, new CTO, new CMO → new tech or new strategy soon.
- They start benchmarking against competitors publicly.
- They begin educating their audience about a topic they previously ignored.
- They adopt specific industry keywords or buzzwords aggressively.
- Shifted Tone: They shift their posting tone from a polished brand voice to problem-focused messaging (e.g., suddenly discussing the complexity of data pipelines).
- Internal Overwork Indicators: Mentions of internal overwork or understaffing from employees -> operational stress and a need for automation/staff augmentation.
- Leadership Reshuffles: Leadership reshuffles (new CTO, new CMO, new Head of Sales) -> almost always signals a new tech stack or new strategy soon.
- Educational Pivot: They begin educating their audience about a technical topic they previously ignored (indicates they are building a product or service in that area).
- Aggressive Keyword Adoption: They start using specific industry keywords or buzzwords aggressively and with high frequency (indicates a sudden internal push or strategic priority).
- Benchmarking: They start benchmarking against competitors publicly or discussing performance gaps (signals a competitive drive to catch up).
- Employee Endorsements: An increase in employees liking/endorsing posts about change initiatives or new product features (signals strong internal commitment).
- Persistent Pain: Repeated posts on the same pain point over several months (indicates a persistent, unresolved issue that needs an external solution).
- Unusually High Activity: Posts about specific challenges generate unusually high comment activity or community responses showing need ("We had the same issue—what did you use to solve it?").
